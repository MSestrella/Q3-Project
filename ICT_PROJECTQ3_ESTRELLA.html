All about Christopher Edwin Breaux

Frank Ocean

Where did the name Frank Ocean comes from? The name was inspired by Frank Sinatra and his 1960 Rat Pack film, Ocean’s 11. While the name itself has simple enough origins, the decision to come up with a stage name was one of necessity. “For me, the genesis of that name change came from me trying to make a project without my label knowing about it. It was born from a need,” Ocean explained to Essence. “I hear certain people say that about their name, their look, even their sound, that it’s a form of armor or a shield from whatever pain they might not want to feel,” he continued. “I don’t think that was it, though. I was just trying to be slick, so I could do what I was doing without people knowing. And it worked.”

Frank Ocean's story

On October 28, 1987, in Long Beach, California, Christopher Edwin Breaux became Frank Ocean, a singer, songwriter, and record producer. Love, identity, and social issues are common themes in Ocean's music, which has earned him praise from critics and a devoted following. He rose to fame in the early 2010s with his mixtape "Nostalgia, Ultra" and his first studio album "Channel Orange." His lyrics are known for their introspective tone and his unique blend of R&B, soul, and alternative music. Ocean is well-known for his involvement outside of the music industry, particularly in support of LGBTQ+ rights.
